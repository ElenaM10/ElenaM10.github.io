Reflections

Introduction to Machine Learning (ML)

I watched the lecture cast on machine learning, which aimed to provide an introduction to the topic. While the lecturecast covered a variety of topics, including the regulation of big data, self-driving cars, and IoT, I found the presentation to be quite vague and disorganized. It seemed more like a collection of machine learning concepts rather than a cohesive overview.

However, I did find the reading list to be more informative. I prefer short articles that can deliver key insights in a straightforward and concise manner, and I found this article by Ayush Pant quite interesting.

The other reading materials were a paper on Correlation and Regression, a chapter in Deep Learning & Social Computing, and An introduction to machine learning Miroslav Kub√°t. While, I understand that the complexity of machine learning requires understanding advanced concepts, I couldn't help but wonder if the reading list belonged to different units in the module.

Exploratory Data Analysis

Week 2 of the module seemed relaxed, and the reading list reflected the same. It was relatively easy and cohesive. This week covered the Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA), and yet again, I found myself leaning towards another article that is part of the reading list.

As we begin getting our hands dirty with Python, it now feels like the machine learning module I was looking forward to. To perform this EDA, I used Jupyter Notebook through the Conda environment (I had previously worked with these tools). You will find all the details of the EDA here.
